Outcomes
Measured outcomes, operational changes, and future success measures

For an NHS enterprise platform, success is not one metric. Some outcomes are directly measured. Some are operational improvements that become visible because manual work disappears or governance failures stop recurring. Others are still future measures because the workstream is active rather than finished.

Operational Outcomes
Changes that matter even when they do not fit a single headline metric
Governance by Design

Restricted permission architecture held from day one. Legacy governance failures were not recreated on the new platform. That matters precisely because it becomes less visible over time.

Administrative Burden

Staff awards nominations moved away from a manual free-text process that required daily ESR cross-referencing and spreadsheet unpicking. Verified staff and team selection reduced avoidable admin effort.

Future Success Measures
Metrics already defined for active workstreams
Staff Awards Nomination System
→Reduction in daily admin time processing nominations — baseline from the previous cycle.
→Reduction in nomination errors — target: zero name verification errors for internal staff.
→Number of nominations submitted — target: match or exceed 2,300 from the previous cycle.
→Staff feedback on ease of use — post-cycle survey.
→Nominee notification rate — all internal nominees receive email confirmation.
ESR Assignment Logic
→Training assignments remain accurate for staff in complex positions, including secondments and restructures.
→No bulk remapping is required when new positions are created for generic mandatory training.
→The audit trail is meaningful — assignments are explained by role and staff group, not only by position number.
→Supplier confirms the hybrid model is technically feasible within the current ESR integration architecture.
