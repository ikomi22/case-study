Outcomes
Measured outcomes, operational changes, and future success measures

For an NHS enterprise platform, success is not one metric. Some outcomes are directly measured. Some are operational improvements that become visible because manual work disappears or governance failures stop recurring. Others are still future measures because the workstream is active rather than finished.

Measured Outcomes — Staff Recognition (OSCAS)

The previous nomination process relied on free-text submission, daily manual ESR cross-referencing, and spreadsheet reconciliation. The figures below come from a structured before/after interview with the People Support team lead responsible for processing nominations, validated against system data on nomination volume.

⏱ Admin Time Saved
2+
Weeks of admin effort cut from the cycle overall, with roughly a couple of days a week saved during the active nomination period — verified names and pull-through removed the bulk of manual correction work.
⚠ Manual Correction Rate — Before
60–70%
Of nominations in the previous cycle required manual name correction or ESR pull-through before a VLOOKUP would resolve. Data quality issues included misspellings, missing capitalisation, and hidden hard spaces.
✓ LMS Adoption Share
78%
Of all nominations received (≈1,328 of ≈1,700) were submitted through the new LMS-based pathway, where employee data is pulled automatically rather than typed as free text.
Methodology note: admin time figures are estimates from a single team lead interview, not a timed study. The interviewee was explicit that she could not verify how much additional admin time a colleague (referred to as Hakeem) spent on the same process, so the total administrative saving across the full team is likely higher than what is captured here. The 60–70% manual correction figure and the adoption share are treated with the same caution — directionally strong, not laboratory-precise.

Operational Outcomes
Changes that matter even when they do not fit a single headline metric
Governance by Design

Restricted permission architecture held from day one. Legacy governance failures were not recreated on the new platform. That matters precisely because it becomes less visible over time.

Administrative Burden — Before / After

Before: the team lead described the nomination period as "practically a full-time role" for several weeks, checking 60–70% of names manually before ESR pull-through would resolve, often acting as "a detective" to identify the right person's department. After: verified names and automatic pull-through removed most of that correction work, saving an estimated couple of days a week during the cycle and a couple of weeks overall.
