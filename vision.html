Product Vision
Living document · Updated 2025
Platform Vision
Our LMS will be measured by how well it helps staff and managers complete, evidence and report workforce learning, appraisal and competency requirements efficiently — while reducing duplication, improving data quality, and removing avoidable administrative effort across people processes. Where appropriate, the platform will also be used to streamline related organisational workflows where structured data, automation and visibility can reduce manual effort and improve consistency.

The percentage of staff who are current with their required learning, appraisal and competency evidence — and whose status is visible and actionable by the appropriate manager.

The following artefacts document the product thinking behind two active workstreams. Each set includes a problem statement, user personas, user stories, acceptance criteria, and a decision log. These are working documents — not retrospective reconstruction.

Workstream 01
Staff Awards Nomination System
Problem Statement

Staff recognition nominations were submitted as free text through a third-party platform, creating significant administrative burden. The People Support team ran daily ESR reports, manually cross-referenced each nomination to identify nominees and nominators, unpicked name errors, and compiled findings into a master spreadsheet. Identifying teams was particularly problematic. Supporting staff employed by third-party contracted employers had no clear nomination pathway despite being longstanding members of the organisation and contributing financially to the awards. With over 2,300 nominations in the last cycle, this process was unsustainable.

User Personas
The Nominator
Goal
Recognise a colleague or team quickly and confidently
Frustration
Typing names from memory, no confirmation received, unclear how to nominate contracted staff
Success
Finds person by searching, submits in under two minutes, receives immediate confirmation
The Administrator
Goal
Process nominations accurately without daily manual unpicking
Frustration
Daily ESR cross-referencing, spreadsheet management, name errors requiring correction, teams impossible to identify
Success
Verified nominations arrive from the system. Daily report generated automatically. Manual spreadsheet work eliminated.
The Nominee
Goal
Know they have been recognised and feel appreciated
Frustration
Currently finds out informally or not at all until the awards ceremony
Success
Receives email confirmation when nominated — recognition felt immediately, not just at the ceremony
User Stories
As an internal staff member, I want to search for and select a colleague or team from the system so that I don't need to remember exact names or spellings.
As an internal staff member, I want to receive confirmation after submitting a nomination so that I know it was received.
As an administrator, I want nominations to arrive with verified employee names and team data so that I don't have to manually cross-reference ESR reports daily.
As an administrator, I want to generate daily nomination reports from the system so that I can present findings without building manual spreadsheets.
As a nominee, I want to receive an email when I am nominated so that I feel recognised before the awards ceremony.
Acceptance Criteria
✓Internal staff can search and select a colleague or team by name from Active Directory or LMS — no free text name entry
✓Nominator receives email confirmation immediately after submission
✓Nominee receives email notification when nominated
✓Introduction page clearly explains how to nominate contracted support staff via the external system
✓Teams can be nominated via LMS accounts bulk uploaded from the mandatory training department list
✓Administrator can generate daily nomination reports from the system
✓External staff nomination pathway remains functional and is communicated clearly on the introduction page
Decision Log
Decision 01 — Team Nominations
Options
Remove team nomination capability entirely, or create dummy LMS accounts representing teams
Decision
Create dummy accounts for teams using bulk upload from mandatory training department list
Why
Teams are a significant nomination category. Removing the option reduces the value of the system.
Trade-off: Introduces dummy accounts into the LMS. Accepted as a deliberate compromise given the value of team recognition.
Decision 02 — LMS vs External System
Options
Continue with third-party platform for all nominations, or move internal nominations to LMS
Decision
Move internal nominations to LMS using Active Directory connection. External contracted staff continue using previous system.
Why
AD connection eliminates free text name entry and manual ESR cross-referencing — the core problem.
Trade-off: Creates a split process — internal via LMS, external via old system. Managed through clear communication on the introduction page.
Decision 03 — Annual vs Monthly Recognition Sequencing
Options
Run annual awards and monthly recognition simultaneously, or sequence them
Decision
Run annual awards first. Edit form for monthly recognition after conclusion. Re-edit for annual awards the following year.
Why
System limitations prevent both running simultaneously. Concurrent forms would create a poor user experience.
Trade-off: Monthly recognition launches later than originally intended. Accepted to protect quality of experience.
Decision 04 — Individual vs Group Nominations
Options
Allow staff to nominate multiple people in one submission, or require individual nominations
Decision
Individual nominations only — one person or team per submission
Why
System functionality does not support multi-person nominations cleanly without compromising data quality.
Trade-off: Additional effort for nominators wanting to recognise multiple people. Mitigated by making each individual nomination quick and simple.
Success Metrics
→Reduction in daily admin time processing nominations — baseline from previous cycle
→Reduction in nomination errors — target: zero name verification errors for internal staff
→Number of nominations submitted — target: match or exceed 2,300 from previous cycle
→Staff feedback on ease of use — post-cycle survey
→Nominee notification rate — all internal nominees receive email confirmation
Workstream 02 · In Progress
Nursing Competency Framework
Problem Statement

Nursing competency requirements are currently fragmented across multiple systems and documents with no single source of truth. Nurses cannot see their full compliance picture in one place. Ward managers cannot report on team competency status meaningfully. The L&D team cannot evidence organisational compliance at scale. This creates risk in a regulated clinical environment where competency evidence is directly linked to patient safety.

Discovery Approach

Unlike the appraisal workstream — where users were not involved early enough — the nursing framework is being approached differently. Discovery conversations with nursing leads are being conducted before any configuration begins. The pilot starts with a single competency to validate the approach before expanding. This is a deliberate application of the lesson learned from the appraisal rollout.

Starting with one competency is not a limitation — it is a product decision. Validate the approach with real users before scaling. The appraisal experience proved why this matters.
User Personas — Draft
The Nurse
Goal
Know what competencies are required and evidence completion in one accessible place
Current frustration
Requirements scattered across multiple documents and systems — no single view
The Ward Manager
Goal
See team compliance status at a glance and act on gaps before they become risks
Current frustration
No unified reporting — compliance picture requires manual compilation from multiple sources
The Nursing Lead
Goal
Evidence organisational nursing competency compliance to regulators and leadership
Current frustration
Fragmented data makes compliance reporting time-consuming and unreliable
Status
✓Problem statement defined
✓User personas drafted — to be validated with nursing leads
→Discovery conversations with nursing leads — in progress
→User stories — pending discovery completion
→Acceptance criteria — pending user story completion
→Single competency pilot — pending discovery sign-off

Discovery · Session 01
AHP Development Framework — Initial Discovery
Context

Allied Health Professionals represent approximately 12 staff groups within the Trust. AHP staff are required to revalidate with the Health and Care Professions Council every two years. There is a national AHP framework structured around four pillars — clinical, education, leadership, and research. This session was the first conversation with the AHP Lead before any wider stakeholder involvement.

The Problem — In Her Words

Graduates with defined career pathways want to progress but there is no infrastructure within the Trust to support that progression. Evidence for revalidation is currently kept informally in personal drives. There is no single place that shows what is expected of an AHP at their particular stage of development. There is no visible connection between the Trust's AHP staff and the national framework.

What Success Looks Like
→Any staff member at any career stage can see what is expected of them at their current level
→Someone wanting to progress can see and identify exactly what they need to do next
→Teams can see the structure they need to create training plans for their staff
→The LMS can accept PDF uploads for HCPC revalidation evidence storage
What We Don't Know Yet
?What the LMS can technically support — supplier conversation required before any commitments
?What the national AHP framework looks like in full and how it maps to the four pillars in practice
?What end users actually need — discovery call with AHP staff being arranged
?Whether PDF upload is feasible within current LMS configuration
?What the blueprint from another NHS Trust looks like in detail — spreadsheet shared but not yet reviewed
Next Steps
→Supplier meeting — understand what is technically possible before any design decisions
→End user discovery call — Michael to be included to set the tone
→Review blueprint spreadsheet from other Trust
→Contact the other Trust's LMS specialist for implementation insights
No configuration decisions will be made before end users have been consulted. The discovery call with AHP staff is a prerequisite to any supplier or build conversation.
Discovery · Session 02
AHP Development Framework — Multi-Stakeholder Discovery
Context

A broader group session with the AHP Lead and leads from multiple AHP workstreams. This session surfaced shared themes, genuine tensions, and a clearer picture of the priority need.

What the Group Said

The therapy lead shared an existing Word document that staff carry across multiple Trusts — a portable record of learning and development gaps. This shows some workstreams already have informal solutions in place. Multiple leads expressed a desire for one place combining appraisals, CPD, revalidation, career logging, and learning resources linked to the four pillars. Career progression visibility was a recurring theme — staff currently have to go to their manager to find out where they are and what they need to do next. The group was clear that course completion should not imply automatic career progression — framing matters as much as functionality.

Tensions Identified
Tension 01 — Scope
The ask
Appraisals, CPD, revalidation, career logging, and learning resources all in one place
The risk
Building too much too soon before proving value with a smaller scope
The principle
Start with the highest priority need, prove value, then expand
Tension 02 — Duplication
The question
Does a competency framework duplicate the appraisal work already being done?
The likely answer
Appraisals as a conversation, the framework as a development reference — different purposes
Status
Unresolved — needs explicit agreement before any build decisions
Tension 03 — Portable vs Platform
The signal
The therapy lead's Word document is working and travels across Trusts
The risk
Building a platform solution that isn't meaningfully better than what already exists
The question
What does the LMS offer that a Word document cannot?
The Priority — What They Said
A single place where a staff member can independently see where they are in their career, what they need to do next, and what learning they need to achieve to grow — without needing to ask their manager.
What Success Looks Like — Emerging Picture
→Staff can independently look up their career position and next steps without asking their manager
→Managers can point staff to a structured reference for development planning
→Learning resources are visible and linked to the four pillars of practice
→The solution sparks development conversations — it does not replace them
→Framing is clear — completing a course does not automatically mean career progression
Next Steps — Agreed
→Speak to the other Trust contact — understand their implementation and lessons learned
→Supplier conversation — understand what the LMS can technically support
→End user survey — wider AHP staff input. Michael to have input into survey questions.
→Return to the group with what is possible and what is not before any build decisions
The priority is clear even if the scope is not. People don't know what's available — and when they find out, there's no structured way to act on it. That is the problem worth solving first.
User Testing · Session 01
Nursing Competency Framework — First Iteration Review
Context

First user testing session with nursing staff on the competency framework pilot. Unlike the appraisal rollout — where users were not involved until training — this workstream has involved users from the pilot stage. This session is Build-Measure-Learn in practice.

Feedback Received
↻Wording on certain buttons needs reviewing — language did not feel natural or clear to users
↻Positioning of certain sections needs adjustment — layout did not match how users expected to navigate
↻Structure of certain sections needs rethinking — organisation of content did not align with how nurses think about their competency requirements
Decisions Made
✓Users will be kept involved and consulted on the next version before it goes live
✓No changes to be made without going back to the user group first
✓Specific wording, positioning, and structure changes to be scoped for iteration two
Next Steps
→Document specific changes needed from session feedback
→Share proposed changes with nursing group before implementing
→Schedule iteration two review session
→Update acceptance criteria to reflect any new requirements surfaced
This is what involving users before full rollout looks like in practice. The pilot surfaces real experience issues that wouldn't have been visible otherwise. The next iteration has a clear brief.

What's Next
Forward direction · 2025–2026

The platform is moving from a delivery-focused programme to a product-led operating model. Each new workstream is being approached with earlier user involvement, defined success metrics, and explicit decision documentation.

Near Term — Optimise

Complete manager reporting and visibility. Resolve hierarchy data accuracy. Enable position number enrolment. Extend ESR API to non-mandatory training. Complete nursing framework pilot and evaluate expansion.

Longer Term — Expand

New starter onboarding pathway. AHP skills framework. Monthly recognition programme. Manager self-service compliance dashboard. Automation of routine enrolment and reporting processes.

Product Operating Principles

→Involve users before the build — not after
→Define success metrics before delivery begins
→Pilot before full rollout — always
→Document decisions with options and trade-offs
→Governance before scale — restrict first, open deliberately
→Adoption is behavioural — not just technical
