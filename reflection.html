07
Reflection
What I would change, what I would protect, and what this work taught me
Would Change
—Start discovery before procurement — validate requirements through user interviews, not only one stakeholder conversation.
—Define explicit success metrics upfront — go-live dates existed, but adoption targets, completion rates, and data quality benchmarks should have been set before delivery.
—Involve end users before the build — ADKAR from day one, not at launch stage.
—Engage Information Governance earlier — caution that arrives late becomes obstruction.
—Build more formal product delivery artefacts where needed. The workplan existed. The next level is product-native artefacts that scale across teams.
Would Start Earlier
—Cross-functional group — used earlier and more deliberately from the beginning.
—Structured stakeholder mapping — surface friction points before they become blockers.
—User research with community teams — before designing for them, not after.
—Product roadmap communication to leadership — visibility of direction reduces surprise and resistance.
—Delegating earlier and more deliberately — named owners existed in the tracker, but ownership culture could have been built faster.
Would Protect
—Pilot before full rollout — always.
—Governance by design — restricted access from the start was right.
—Post go-live feedback mechanisms — feedback should not stop at pilot.
—Going physically to community teams — meet people where they are.
—Cross-functional task and finish groups — just involve them earlier.
07b
The Five Product Lessons
What this work changed in how I think

Before this role I thought product work was about managing and owning a product. I understand now it is also about the relationships around it: the people who commission it, build it, govern it, use it, and resist it. Those relationships determine whether the product succeeds or fails.

01
TrustBeforeTooling
You cannot build on a broken foundation. Stabilise first. Earn trust before asking people to adopt something new.
02
GovernanceBeforeScale
A system that starts restricted can be opened deliberately. A system that starts open accumulates problems that are much harder to unpick later.
03
StabiliseBeforeTransform
Discovery is not a luxury. It is the difference between solving the real problem and solving what someone thought the problem was.
04
AdoptionIsBehavioural
You cannot train your way out of a desire problem. Awareness and desire are built before the product is built — not after launch.
05
ProductIsRelationships
In every case — the staff member, the pharmacy director, the community teams — the answer was not only a better product. It was a better relationship around the product.
A developer asks

How do I build this?

A product manager asks

Should I build this — and if so, for whom, and how will I know if it worked?
