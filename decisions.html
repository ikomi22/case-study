The Decisions
Seven moments · Problem → Decision → Outcome
Decision 01
Stabilisation First
Fix what's broken before building what's new
Problem
Staff using a broken system daily while new platform is being procured
Constraint
Cannot build trust in something new while the existing thing is visibly failing
Decision
Fix legacy LMS first — SSO, completions, pathways, user guides
Outcome
Foundation of trust restored before new platform launched

This was a shared decision made in an early meeting with my managers — I want to be honest about that. What I brought to that conversation was both the clarity about why it mattered and the structured workplan that gave the decision a delivery framework. A stable foundation isn't a delay — it's a prerequisite.

Decision 02
G-Cloud Procurement
Choosing familiarity over modernity
Problem
Contract expiry on legacy LMS. Need a new platform — fast, governed, right
Process
G-Cloud framework · Scoring matrix · 4 suppliers · Written bids + live demos
Decision
Incumbent supplier recommended — existing ICT relationship, known data architecture
Outcome
Fast implementation, reduced integration risk, smooth SSO from day one
What we traded away
A more modern-looking platform from a strong contender — fresher UI, more contemporary UX that might have driven stronger initial engagement.
What we gained
Speed of implementation, existing ICT familiarity, known data architecture, reduced risk at a high-pressure moment with a hard deadline.
Decision 03
Migration Approach
Migration is not copying
Problem
300–500 courses, dirty data, fake accounts, broken pathways — what moves and what doesn't?
Constraint
Contract expiry June 2025 — migrate or lose it. Migrating everything = migrating every problem. Compliance data must be preserved.
Decision
Deliberate curation — left dirty data behind. Every SCORM/AICC tested. All reports backed up.
Outcome
Clean platform, zero legacy governance failures replicated, historical data preserved

What was deliberately left behind: Room accounts. Outdated courses. Incorrectly structured pathways rebuilt from scratch. Edge cases fixed — student nurses, maternity returners, people outside the standard data pipeline — all given documented workarounds that the legacy system never resolved.

Governance by design: The permission architecture was designed restrictively from day one — agreed with my managers as a deliberate quality decision. You can always open a system that starts restricted. You cannot easily fix a system that started open and accumulated years of ungoverned content.

Every decision was a trade-off between speed and integrity. Integrity won.
Decision 04
Pilot Design — 12 Months of Iteration
Not launching until the product had earned trust
Problem
Moving 13,000+ staff from paper to digital — too high stakes to launch unvalidated
Method
Open pilot · Monthly analysis · Copilot on anonymised feedback · Cross-functional group
Key Change
Objectives section redesigned — space to reflect on prior goals before new framework
Outcome
Staff described new process as easier and faster — consensus to launch after 12 months

Discovery before build: Before a single element of the appraisal was configured on the LMS, I met with the People Promise Manager — the person who owned the previous appraisal process. She understood the feedback and lived experience of staff better than anyone. What she described was a Word document that had become a source of frustration — when staff typed into it, the formatting broke, fields moved, and the document became unpredictable to complete. That conversation shaped everything that followed. As an interim fix while the LMS version was being built, I patched the Word document and returned it — reducing the immediate pain while the proper solution was in progress. That instinct to stabilise the existing process while designing the replacement is the same principle that drove the platform work from the start.

Responsible AI use in practice: personal identifiers manually removed from every feedback report before Copilot extracted thematic analysis. That's not a principle to cite. It's a practice to embed.

In a training session, a staff member stood up and told me directly he wasn't going to use the system. His resistance wasn't about the technology. It was about not feeling consulted. You cannot train your way out of a desire problem.
Decision 05
Rollout Strategy
Taking the product to the people
Problem
Digital communication alone won't drive adoption — desire problem identified
Method
Multi-channel: comms, intranet, SharePoint hub, physical roadshow over 2 weeks
Roadshow
Banners, flyers, values spinning wheel (team idea), cards to SharePoint hub
Outcome
95–98% appraisal usage uplift in roadshow week — system data, not a survey

The rollout was a team effort — coordinated across the L&D team with named owners for each channel. The roadshow was planned collaboratively, with team members empowered to bring their own ideas. The spinning wheel tied to organisational values came from a colleague — that kind of distributed creative ownership is what made the roadshow feel human rather than corporate.

The Director of Pharmacy pushed back — her team was navigating too much change at once. What moved her wasn't a feature list. It was peer evidence: colleagues across the organisation describing the new process as easier and faster than paper. Peer experience is more convincing than any pitch.

Decision 06
Permission Architecture

Access designed restrictively from day one — not to gatekeep, but because I had seen what happens when nobody does. Gradual expansion is ongoing. Governance by design is not bureaucracy. It is the difference between a platform that stays coherent and one that becomes ungovernable.

Decision 07
Community Teams

Community nurses and AHPs operate outside the main compound — physically and culturally separate. Standard rollout wouldn't land. We went to them: in-person, bespoke, on their site. Gradual but measurable increase in appraisal completions followed. User segmentation is the practical recognition that different people need different things.
