03
Strategy
From programme recovery to product direction

The platform is moving from a delivery-focused programme to a product-led operating model. The strategy was not to do everything at once. It was to stabilise the foundation, make the system governable, deliver the highest-risk workflows first, and only then expand into adjacent workforce problems where the platform could reduce duplication and administrative effort.

03b
Product Direction
Structured workplan, priorities, and workstreams

Before any configuration began, the work was mapped into a structured tracker built collaboratively with my managers. It captured workstreams, key considerations, named owners, target dates, RAG status, priority levels, and approval gates. Ownership was distributed deliberately across the team and wider stakeholders rather than concentrated in one person.

Prioritisation decisions were made explicitly. Recognition and the nursing framework were sequenced after the foundation workstreams — migration, the ESR mandatory training link, and appraisals — because expanding the platform before stabilising it would have replicated the governance failures we were trying to move away from.

Two milestones anchored the appraisal workstream from the outset: a go-live target at the start of 2026, and a new appraisal template ready by the end of tax year April 2026. Those were delivery commitments, but they also disciplined the product decisions around pilot length, iteration, and rollout readiness.

03c
Dependencies and Sequencing
The enterprise logic behind the roadmap

ESR is a nationally mandated system that is not going anywhere. It houses salaries, annual leave, employment records, and personal data. No Trust, however forward-thinking, replaces it casually. That meant the product strategy had to be integration-aware from the start.

The legacy LMS contract expired in June 2025. That was a hard stop. Training had to move to the new LMS before the ESR API integration was complete. Waiting for an ideal architecture would have left the organisation without a functioning platform.

For the interim period, staff completed mandatory training on the new LMS while ESR had no automated way of knowing. Rolling banners and structured communications managed the gap. Eventually the API was configured for mandatory training. Other courses still required a daily manual upload. Not elegant. But workable.

Operating Model
How the platform is being run now
Strategic Bets — Now

Manager Visibility — give managers direct access to team readiness data. Workforce Automation — eliminate manual enrolment and ESR upload processes. Complete nursing framework iteration two with users. Resolve manager hierarchy data and DPIA dependencies.

Strategic Bets — Next

Career Development — AHP framework, ACP portfolio, nursing expansion. Workforce Experience — new starter pathway, monthly recognition, volunteer access. Generate Workforce Insight — skills heat maps, pillar-level reporting, learning demand forecasting.

Product Operating Principles

→Involve users before the build — not after
→Define success metrics before delivery begins
→Pilot before full rollout — always
→Document decisions with options and trade-offs
→Governance before scale — restrict first, open deliberately
→Adoption is behavioural — not just technical
