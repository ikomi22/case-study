05
Discovery
Live product work, uncertainty, and how assumptions changed

The discovery approach changed over time. In the appraisal workstream, users were not involved early enough. Later workstreams were deliberately handled differently: earlier conversations, clearer unknowns, fewer assumptions hidden as certainty, and a stronger link between discovery and the next decision.

Before a single element of the appraisal was configured on the LMS, I met with the People Promise Manager who owned the previous process. She described a Word document that broke when staff typed into it. That conversation shaped the digital design and prompted an interim fix to reduce pain while the replacement was being built.

The artefacts on this page are working documents rather than retrospective reconstruction. They show the product thinking as it happened, including what was still unknown.

Discovery Principles
Users First
Rule
No configuration decisions before speaking to the people affected where that conversation had not happened already.
Unknowns Visible
Rule
What we did not know was documented openly rather than disguised as confidence.
Pilot Small
Rule
Start with one competency or one workflow where necessary, validate it, then scale from evidence.
Iterate Publicly
Rule
Feedback, changes, and unresolved tensions stay visible so the product does not become a fiction of perfect foresight.

Workstream 01
Staff Awards Nomination System
Problem Statement

Staff recognition nominations were submitted as free text through a third-party platform, creating significant administrative burden. The People Support team ran daily ESR reports, manually cross-referenced each nomination to identify nominees and nominators, unpicked name errors, and compiled findings into a master spreadsheet. Identifying teams was particularly problematic. Supporting staff employed by third-party contracted employers had no clear nomination pathway despite being longstanding members of the organisation and contributing financially to the awards. With over 2,300 nominations in the last cycle, this process was unsustainable.

User Personas
The Nominator
Goal
Recognise a colleague or team quickly and confidently
Frustration
Typing names from memory, no confirmation received, unclear how to nominate contracted staff
Success
Finds the right person by searching, submits in under two minutes, receives immediate confirmation
The Administrator
Goal
Process nominations accurately without daily manual unpicking
Frustration
Daily ESR cross-referencing, spreadsheet management, name errors requiring correction, teams impossible to identify
Success
Verified nominations arrive from the system. Daily report is generated automatically. Manual spreadsheet work is eliminated.
The Nominee
Goal
Know they have been recognised and feel appreciated
Frustration
Currently finds out informally or not at all until the awards ceremony
Success
Receives email confirmation when nominated — recognition is felt immediately, not only at the ceremony
User Stories
As an internal staff member, I want to search for and select a colleague or team from the system so that I do not need to remember exact names or spellings.
As an internal staff member, I want to receive confirmation after submitting a nomination so that I know it was received.
As an administrator, I want nominations to arrive with verified employee names and team data so that I do not have to manually cross-reference ESR reports daily.
As an administrator, I want to generate daily nomination reports from the system so that I can present findings without building manual spreadsheets.
As a nominee, I want to receive an email when I am nominated so that I feel recognised before the awards ceremony.
Acceptance Criteria
✓Internal staff can search and select a colleague or team by name from Active Directory or LMS — no free text name entry.
✓Nominator receives email confirmation immediately after submission.
✓Nominee receives email notification when nominated.
✓Introduction page clearly explains how to nominate contracted support staff via the external system.
✓Teams can be nominated via LMS accounts bulk uploaded from the mandatory training department list.
✓Administrator can generate daily nomination reports from the system.
✓External staff nomination pathway remains functional and is communicated clearly on the introduction page.
Decision Log
Decision 01 — Team Nominations
Options
Remove team nominations entirely, or create dummy LMS accounts representing teams.
Decision
Create dummy accounts for teams using a bulk upload from the mandatory training department list.
Why
Teams are a significant nomination category. Removing the option would reduce the value of the system.
Trade-off: introduces dummy accounts into the LMS. Accepted because the value of team recognition outweighed the compromise.
Decision 02 — LMS vs External System
Options
Keep everything in the third-party platform, or move internal nominations into LMS.
Decision
Move internal nominations into LMS using the Active Directory connection. External contracted staff continue on the previous route.
Why
The Active Directory connection eliminates free text name entry and manual ESR cross-referencing, which was the core problem.
Trade-off: creates a split process — internal through LMS, external through the previous system. Managed through clear communication.
Decision 03 — Annual vs Monthly Recognition Sequencing
Options
Run annual awards and monthly recognition at the same time, or sequence them.
Decision
Run annual awards first, then repurpose the form for monthly recognition afterwards.
Why
System limitations made simultaneous forms a poor experience.
Trade-off: monthly recognition launches later than originally intended. Accepted to protect quality of experience.
Decision 04 — Individual vs Group Nominations
Options
Allow one submission for multiple people, or keep nominations individual.
Decision
One person or one team per submission.
Why
The system could not support multi-person nominations cleanly without compromising data quality.
Trade-off: more effort for staff nominating several people. Mitigated by making each individual nomination quick and simple.
Success Metrics — Results
✓Admin time processing nominations — reduced by an estimated couple of days a week during the cycle and a couple of weeks overall, against a previous baseline described as "practically a full-time role."
✓Nomination errors — manual name/pull-through correction fell from an estimated 60–70% of nominations to a small residual rate on LMS-submitted nominations.
✓Nominations submitted — approximately 1,700 received, with 1,328 (78%) via the LMS pathway, matching the scale of the previous cycle's 2,300+ while shifting most volume onto the verified route.
→Staff feedback on ease of use — post-cycle survey still to run.
→Nominee notification rate — all internal nominees receive email confirmation.
Source and caveat: these results come from a structured interview with the People Support team lead who processed nominations in both cycles. She was explicit that she could not verify how much time a colleague spent on the same process, so total team-wide admin savings are likely understated here rather than overstated.
Workstream 02 · In Progress
Nursing Competency Framework
Problem Statement

Nursing competency requirements are currently fragmented across multiple systems and documents with no single source of truth. Nurses cannot see their full compliance picture in one place. Ward managers cannot report on team competency status meaningfully. The L&D team cannot evidence organisational compliance at scale. This creates risk in a regulated clinical environment where competency evidence is directly linked to patient safety.

Discovery Approach

Unlike the appraisal workstream — where users were not involved early enough — the nursing framework is being approached differently. Discovery conversations with nursing leads were conducted before any wider configuration began. The pilot starts with a single competency to validate the approach before expanding. This is a deliberate application of the lesson learned from the appraisal rollout.

Starting with one competency is not a limitation — it is a product decision. Validate the approach with real users before scaling. The appraisal experience proved why this matters.
Product Insight — Assumption Challenged

Initial discussions with stakeholders focused heavily on competency evidence storage — where paper and digital records should live. Discovery conversations with Nursing Education Leads revealed something different: the primary problem was not evidence upload itself, but the absence of a Trust-wide view of nursing competency. Evidence currently sits fragmented across paper records, local ward files, manager-held documents, and personal drives, with no single source of truth and no consistent reporting mechanism.

The core need that emerged is visibility, governance, assurance, and reporting. Evidence capture remains an important supporting capability, but it is not the primary outcome nursing leads are seeking — a distinction that would not have surfaced without discovery happening before build decisions.

The assumption going in was "this is a storage problem." Discovery showed it is a visibility and governance problem, with storage as one supporting piece rather than the goal. That distinction changes what "done" looks like for this workstream.
User Personas — Updated From Discovery
The Nurse
Goal
Know what competencies are required and evidence completion in one accessible place.
Current frustration
Evidence is scattered across paper records, ward files, manager-held documents, and personal drives — inaccessible when staff move roles or managers change.
The Ward Manager
Goal
See team compliance status at a glance and act on gaps before they become risks.
Current frustration
No single source of truth or Trust-wide view means limited visibility of team competency status and difficulty tracking compliance consistently.
The Nursing Lead
Goal
Evidence organisational nursing competency compliance to regulators and leadership.
Current frustration
No Trust-wide reporting capability or consistent mechanism, limiting organisational assurance and governance oversight.
Status
✓Problem statement defined.
✓User personas drafted and updated following discovery conversations with nursing leads.
✓Discovery conversations with Nursing Education Leads — complete. Core need reframed from evidence storage to Trust-wide visibility, governance, and reporting.
→User stories — being drafted against the reframed problem.
→Acceptance criteria — pending user story completion.
→Single competency pilot — in progress, see Discovery Log.

Discovery · Session 01
AHP Development Framework — Initial Discovery
Context

Allied Health Professionals represent approximately 12 staff groups within the Trust. AHP staff are required to revalidate with the Health and Care Professions Council every two years. There is a national AHP framework structured around four pillars — clinical, education, leadership, and research. This session was the first conversation with the AHP Lead before any wider stakeholder involvement.

The Problem — In Her Words

Graduates with defined career pathways want to progress but there is no infrastructure within the Trust to support that progression. Evidence for revalidation is currently kept informally in personal drives. There is no single place that shows what is expected of an AHP at their particular stage of development. There is no visible connection between the Trust's AHP staff and the national framework.

What Success Looks Like
→Any staff member at any career stage can see what is expected at their current level.
→Someone wanting to progress can identify exactly what they need to do next.
→Teams can see the structure they need to create development plans for staff.
→The LMS can accept PDF uploads for HCPC revalidation evidence storage.
What We Don't Know Yet
?What the LMS can technically support — supplier conversation required before commitments.
?What the national AHP framework looks like in full and how it maps to the four pillars in practice.
?What end users actually need — a discovery call with AHP staff was still being arranged.
?Whether PDF upload is feasible within the current LMS configuration.
?What the blueprint from another NHS Trust looks like in detail.
Next Steps
→Supplier meeting — understand what is technically possible before any design decision.
→End user discovery call — Michael to be included to set the tone.
→Review the blueprint spreadsheet from another Trust.
→Contact the other Trust's LMS specialist for implementation insights.
No configuration decisions will be made before end users have been consulted. The discovery call with AHP staff is a prerequisite to any supplier or build conversation.
Discovery · Session 02
AHP Development Framework — Multi-Stakeholder Discovery
Context

A broader group session with the AHP Lead and leads from multiple AHP workstreams. This session surfaced shared themes, genuine tensions, and a clearer picture of the priority need.

What the Group Said

The therapy lead shared an existing Word document that staff carry across multiple Trusts — a portable record of learning and development gaps. Multiple leads expressed a desire for one place combining appraisals, CPD, revalidation, career logging, and learning resources linked to the four pillars. Career progression visibility was a recurring theme — staff currently have to go to their manager to find out where they are and what they need to do next. The group was clear that course completion should not imply automatic career progression.

Tensions Identified
Tension 01 — Scope
The ask
Appraisals, CPD, revalidation, career logging, and learning resources all in one place.
The risk
Building too much too soon before proving value with a smaller scope.
The principle
Start with the highest priority need, prove value, then expand.
Tension 02 — Duplication
The question
Does a competency framework duplicate the appraisal work already being done?
The likely answer
Appraisals are a conversation; the framework is a development reference. They do not have to be the same thing.
Status
Unresolved — needs explicit agreement before any build decisions.
Tension 03 — Portable vs Platform
The signal
The therapy lead's Word document already works and travels across Trusts.
The risk
Building a platform solution that is not meaningfully better than what already exists.
The question
What does the LMS offer that a Word document cannot?
The Priority — What They Said
A single place where a staff member can independently see where they are in their career, what they need to do next, and what learning they need to achieve to grow — without needing to ask their manager.
What Success Looks Like — Emerging Picture
→Staff can independently look up their career position and next steps without asking their manager.
→Managers can point staff to a structured reference for development planning.
→Learning resources are visible and linked to the four pillars of practice.
→The solution sparks development conversations rather than replacing them.
→Framing is clear — completing a course does not automatically mean career progression.
Next Steps — Agreed
→Speak to the other Trust contact and understand their implementation.
→Have a supplier conversation about what the LMS can technically support.
→Run an end user survey with wider AHP staff input.
→Return to the group with what is possible and what is not before any build decisions.
The priority is clear even if the scope is not. People do not know what is available — and when they find out, there is no structured way to act on it. That is the problem worth solving first.
Discovery · Session 03
Nursing Competency Framework — Nursing Education Leads Discovery
Context

Discovery sessions with Nursing Education Leads were conducted to understand the underlying problem before wider implementation of the nursing competency workstream — applying the same before-build discipline used on the AHP framework rather than repeating the appraisal workstream's late user involvement.

Current State

Competency evidence is currently fragmented across paper records, local ward files, manager-held documents, and personal drives. There is no Trust-wide competency record or consistent reporting mechanism.

Key Pain Points Identified
✗No single source of truth for nursing competency records.
✗Limited visibility for managers.
✗Difficulty tracking compliance consistently across services.
✗Evidence can become inaccessible when staff move roles or managers change.
✗Limited organisational assurance and governance oversight.
The Reframe

Initial discussions focused heavily on competency evidence storage. Discovery with nursing leads revealed the primary problem was not evidence upload itself, but the absence of a Trust-wide view of nursing competency. The core need is visibility, governance, assurance, and reporting — evidence capture is an important supporting capability, but not the primary outcome being sought.

This is the same discipline applied in the AHP workstream — surface the real need through conversation before assuming the shape of the solution. Here it produced a genuine reframe: from "where do we store evidence" to "how does the Trust see and govern competency at all."
Expected Outcomes — For Nurses
→Clearer expectations around competence and development requirements.
→Easier access to competency records.
→Reduced reliance on paper-based processes.
Expected Outcomes — For Managers
→Increased visibility of team competency status.
→Improved development conversations.
→Faster identification of competency gaps.
Expected Outcomes — For Nursing Education Teams
→Trust-wide reporting capability.
→Improved compliance visibility.
→Stronger governance and assurance.
→Reduced fragmentation of competency information.
Next Steps
→Translate the reframed need — visibility, governance, assurance, reporting — into user stories and acceptance criteria.
→Confirm reporting requirements with Nursing Education Leads before scoping the single-competency pilot build.
→Feed findings into the iteration-two review already underway with pilot users.
User Testing · Session 01
Nursing Competency Framework — First Iteration Review
Context

First user testing session with nursing staff on the competency framework pilot. Unlike the appraisal rollout — where users were not involved until training — this workstream involved users from the pilot stage. This session is Build-Measure-Learn in practice.

Feedback Received
↻Wording on certain buttons needed reviewing because the language did not feel natural or clear.
↻Positioning of certain sections needed adjustment because the layout did not match how users expected to navigate.
↻Structure of certain sections needed rethinking because the organisation of content did not match how nurses think about competency requirements.
Decisions Made
✓Users will be kept involved and consulted on the next version before it goes live.
✓No changes will be made without going back to the user group first.
✓Specific wording, positioning, and structure changes will be scoped for iteration two.
Next Steps
→Document specific changes needed from session feedback.
→Share proposed changes with the nursing group before implementing.
→Schedule an iteration-two review session.
→Update acceptance criteria to reflect any new requirements surfaced.
This is what involving users before full rollout looks like in practice. The pilot surfaces real experience issues that would not have been visible otherwise.
