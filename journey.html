From Vision to Structured Workplan

Before any configuration began, the programme was mapped into a structured project tracker — built collaboratively with my managers during early planning sessions. The tracker captured workstreams, key considerations, named owners, target dates, RAG status, priority levels, and approval gates. Ownership was distributed deliberately across the team and wider stakeholders — not concentrated in one person.

Prioritisation decisions were made explicitly. Recognition and the nursing framework were sequenced after the foundation workstreams — migration, mandatory training ESR link, and appraisals — because expanding the platform before stabilising it would have replicated the governance failures we were trying to move away from. That sequencing wasn't accidental. It was a deliberate product decision made at the start of the programme.

Two delivery milestones anchored the appraisal workstream from the outset: a go-live target at the start of 2026, and a new appraisal template ready by end of tax year April 2026. Those commitments were made upfront and tracked throughout — not recognised after the fact.

The Constraint You Can't Remove — ESR

ESR is a nationally mandated system that isn't going anywhere. It houses salaries, annual leave, employment records, and personal data — it is the spine of NHS workforce administration. No individual Trust, however forward-thinking, is replacing it.

The contract on the legacy LMS expired June 2025. That wasn't a soft target — it was a hard stop. Training had to move to the new LMS before the ESR API integration was complete. The decision was deliberate — waiting risked the organisation losing its primary platform with nothing functional to replace it.

For the interim period, staff were completing mandatory training on the new LMS while ESR had no automated way of knowing. A rolling banner on both systems acknowledged the delay. Structured communications managed the gap. Eventually the API was configured — but only for mandatory training. Other courses required a daily manual admin upload. Not elegant. But it worked.
